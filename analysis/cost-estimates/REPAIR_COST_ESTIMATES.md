| Scenario | Low | Average | High |
|----------|-----|---------|------|
| Basic removal/trapping only | $200 | $450 | $700 |
| Removal + exclusion (sealing) | $500 | $1,000 | $1,500 |
| Full remediation (cleanup + exclusion) | $800 | $2,500 | $5,000 |
| Complete restoration (new insulation) | $3,000 | $4,500 | $7,000+ |

### What the Work Involves

**Basic Removal:**
- Inspection to identify entry points and infestation level
- Trapping/baiting program (typically 2-4 weeks)
- Follow-up visits to remove traps

**Full Remediation:**
- Everything above, plus:
- Sealing all entry points (rodent proofing)
- Removal of contaminated insulation
- Sanitizing/disinfecting affected areas
- Hazardous waste disposal

**Complete Restoration:**
- Everything above, plus:
- Installation of new attic insulation

### Factors That Could Increase/Decrease Cost
- **Increases:** Large attic, severe infestation, extensive damage to insulation/wiring, need for electrical repairs (rodent-chewed wires), asbestos present in old insulation
- **Decreases:** Minor infestation caught early, limited to small area, existing insulation in good condition

### Timeline
- Basic removal: 2-4 weeks
- Full remediation: 1-3 days of work
- Complete restoration: 2-5 days

### Permit Requirements
- Generally no permit required
- Electrical repairs if needed require permit

### Bay Area Specific Notes
- Older Bay Area homes have more entry points
- Roof rats and Norway rats common in Alameda area
- Multiple vendors specialize in this work (EcoRattic, Alley Cat, Attics Express)

### Important Recommendation
Start with a professional inspection ($100-$200) to assess the extent before committing to full remediation. The scope varies dramatically based on infestation severity.

## 4. Electrical Upgrades (GFCI and AFCI Protection)

**Issue:** GFCI and AFCI protection additions needed

### Cost Estimates **[RESEARCH ESTIMATE]**

| Scenario | Low | Average | High |
|----------|-----|---------|------|
| Per GFCI outlet (replace existing) | $90 | $150 | $200 |
| Per GFCI outlet (new installation) | $150 | $250 | $350 |
| Per AFCI breaker | $140 | $200 | $350 |
| Estimated total (3-4 GFCI + 2-3 AFCI) | $500 | $1,000 | $2,000 |

### What the Work Involves

**GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter):**
- Typically required in kitchens, bathrooms, garages, outdoor areas, and near water sources
- Can protect downstream outlets on same circuit
- Replacement of existing outlets or new installation

**AFCI (Arc Fault Circuit Interrupter):**
- Required for bedroom circuits (and increasingly living spaces)
- Installed at breaker panel
- Protects against arc faults that can cause fires

### Factors That Could Increase/Decrease Cost
- **Increases:** Old wiring requiring upgrades, panel capacity issues, difficult access, extensive coverage needed
- **Decreases:** Bundling multiple installations in one visit, simple replacements of existing outlets

### Timeline
- Per outlet: 10-30 minutes (replacement) to 1-3 hours (new installation)
- Typical project: Half day to full day

### Permit Requirements
- Permit typically required for new circuits or panel work
- May not be required for simple outlet replacements
- Typical electrical permit: $50-$150

### Cost-Saving Tips
- Schedule multiple outlets for same service call
- GFCI outlets protect downstream outlets, so strategic placement reduces number needed
- Get multiple quotes from licensed electricians

- [HomeGuide - GFCI Outlet Cost](https://homeguide.com/costs/gfci-outlet-cost)

- Check for obvious damage or blockages

**Level 2 (Recommended):**
- Everything in Level 1, plus:

### Permit Requirements
- No permit for inspection
- Repairs may require permit depending on scope

### Important Notes
- Annual chimney inspection recommended by NFPA
- Combined inspection + cleaning: $200-$400
- If flue damage found, repair costs can range from $500-$3,000+

## 6. Water Heater Seismic Strap

**Issue:** Lower strap needs repositioning

### Cost Estimates **[RESEARCH ESTIMATE]**

| Scenario | Low | Average | High |
|----------|-----|---------|------|
| DIY kit | $15 | $30 | $50 |
| Professional repositioning | $100 | $175 | $350 |

### What the Work Involves
- Inspection of current strapping configuration
- Repositioning lower strap to proper location (lower 1/3 of tank)
- Ensuring proper stud anchoring
- Verifying top strap is also correctly positioned (upper 1/3)

### California Code Requirements
- Two metal straps required: upper 1/3 and lower 1/3 of tank
- Straps must be anchored to wall studs or masonry
- Required by California Plumbing Code Section 507.2
- Must be certified at time of property sale

### Factors That Could Increase/Decrease Cost
- **Increases:** Poor stud access, need for additional blocking, full strap replacement needed
- **Decreases:** Simple adjustment, DIY if comfortable with tools

### Timeline
- Professional service: 30-60 minutes
- Often combined with other plumbing work for efficiency

### Permit Requirements
- No permit typically required for strap adjustment
- May be inspected as part of water heater permit if replacing heater

- [California Seismic Safety Commission - Water Heater Guidelines](https://ssc.ca.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/9/2020/08/waterheaterbracing_08-11-04.pdf)

## 7. Gas Drip Leg Installation

**Issue:** Missing drip legs on water heater and furnace

### Cost Estimates **[RESEARCH ESTIMATE]**

| Scenario | Low | Average | High |
|----------|-----|---------|------|
| Per drip leg (materials) | $10 | $15 | $25 |
| Per drip leg (installed) | $100 | $200 | $300 |
| Two drip legs (water heater + furnace) | $200 | $400 | $600 |

### What the Work Involves
- Shutting off gas supply
- Installing tee fitting in gas line before appliance
- Adding nipple and cap to create sediment trap
- Reconnecting appliance
- Testing for gas leaks
- Restoring gas service

### Code Requirements
- Required by International Fuel Gas Code (IFGC) and NFPA 54
- Must be installed on gas furnaces, boilers, and water heaters
- Not required for dryers, ranges, or fireplaces

### Factors That Could Increase/Decrease Cost
- **Increases:** Difficult access, old/corroded fittings, need for additional gas line work
- **Decreases:** Bundling with other plumbing work, easy access

### Timeline
- Per appliance: 30-60 minutes
- Both appliances: 1-2 hours

### Permit Requirements
- May require permit for gas work (check with Alameda Building Department)
- Typical gas permit: $50-$100

### Important Safety Note
Gas work should be performed by licensed professionals. Improper installation can cause gas leaks and safety hazards.
