## 1. COMPLIANCE ITEMS - Seller Obligations Before Close

### Water-Conserving Plumbing Fixtures (WCMD & KW Advisory)
**Status:** SIGNED by both sellers (9/12/2025)

**California Law Requirements (Civil Code 1101.4-1101.5):**
- Property built on or before January 1, 1994 must have water-conserving plumbing fixtures
- Single-family residences compliance deadline: January 1, 2017

**Noncompliant Fixtures Defined:**
- Toilets using >1.6 gallons per flush
- Showerheads with flow >2.5 gallons per minute
- Interior faucets emitting >2.2 gallons per minute

**Seller Disclosure Obligation:**
- Sellers must disclose whether property includes any noncompliant fixtures
- No state law requirement to install compliant fixtures as condition of sale
- Local ordinances may have stricter requirements

**BUYER ACTION REQUIRED:**
- Verify compliance status on TDS (Transfer Disclosure Statement)
- Investigate cost to bring any noncompliant fixtures into compliance before removing contingency
- Check with Alameda building department for local point-of-sale requirements

### Carbon Monoxide Detectors (WCMD)
**Status:** SIGNED by both sellers (9/12/2025)

**Requirements (Health & Safety Code 13260-13263, 17926-17926.2):**
- Required since January 1, 2013 for dwelling units with:
  - Fossil fuel burning heater or appliance
  - Fireplace
  - Attached garage

**Placement Requirements:**
- Outside each sleeping area
- On each floor in multi-level dwelling

**BUYER ACTION REQUIRED:**
- Verify CO detectors are installed and operational
- Seller required to disclose on TDS whether unit has CO detector
- Property has a gas fireplace (per flyer), so CO detectors ARE required

### Smoke Detectors

- Written statement of compliance to be delivered to Buyer

### Water Heater Strapping

- If professionals recommend further inspections, hire qualified experts
- Retain own professionals even if Seller provides existing reports
- Read all written reports and discuss with preparers

**Categories Requiring Investigation (per SBSA):**

| Category | Items to Investigate |
|----------|---------------------|
| Physical Condition | Easements, environmental hazards, mold, geologic conditions, water intrusion, wood destroying pests |
| Property Use | Building permits, zoning compliance, ADU regulations, HOA restrictions |
| Neighborhood | Schools, crime, noise sources, wildlife, underground pipelines |
| Legal | Earthquake zones, flood zones, fire hazards, Megan's Law database |
| Contract | Liquidated damages, arbitration, mediation requirements |

**Specific Investigation Areas:**

1. **Square Footage/Lot Size:** Only appraiser or surveyor can reliably confirm; MLS data often approximations
2. **Schools:** School assignment may vary; contact district to verify
3. **Fire Hazards:** Check if in State Fire Responsibility Area or Very High Fire Hazard Zone
4. **Flood Hazards:** Check FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area designation
5. **Earthquake Zones:** Check Earthquake Fault Zones and Seismic Hazard Zones
6. **Insurance:** May have difficulty obtaining if prior claims on property
7. **HOA:** Review all governing documents, CC&Rs, financial statements, reserves

**Right to Request Repairs:**
- Buyer may request repairs/corrections based on inspections
- Seller is NOT OBLIGATED to respond or make repairs
- If Seller unwilling, Buyer may cancel within contingency periods
