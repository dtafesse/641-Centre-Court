## Harbor Bay Isle Community Response

The Harbor Bay Isle community has been proactive about safety:

1. **Private Security Force:** Harbor Bay Isle maintains its own private security patrol for the 917-acre planned community.

2. **FLOCK Safety Devices:** In 2024, the community began considering adding FLOCK Safety license plate reader cameras to assist security operations.

3. **Police Collaboration:** Alameda Police Lieutenant Josh Crossley met with HOA representatives to discuss collaboration between APD and HBI private security.

4. **Community Beat Meetings:** The Alameda Police Department hosts regular community meetings for residents to engage directly with officers.

## Types of Crime: Property vs. Violent

## Notable Safety Positives

1. **Island Geography:** Bay Farm Island is connected to mainland by limited access points, creating natural barriers to crime.

2. **Affluent Community:** Higher-income areas typically have lower crime rates; Bay Farm Island is in top 15% nationally for income.

3. **HOA Security:** Harbor Bay Isle has dedicated private security in addition to Alameda Police.

4. **Improving Trends:** City-wide crime dropped significantly in 2024, especially property crimes.

5. **Community Engagement:** Active HOA involvement in crime prevention and police collaboration.

---

## Notable Safety Concerns

1. **Vehicle Theft (City-wide):** While improving, Alameda historically has had elevated auto theft rates. Always use garage parking.

2. **Property Crime Rate:** Still above national average for Alameda overall, though Bay Farm Island is better.

3. **Proximity to Oakland:** While Bay Farm Island is insulated, the broader region has higher crime.

4. **Cost of Crime:** CrimeGrade estimates $707/resident annual crime cost for Bay Farm Island ($243 above national average).

## Conclusion

**Is Harbor Bay Isle / Bay Farm Island Safe for the Bay Area?**

**Yes, relatively speaking.** Bay Farm Island is:
- Safer than the broader Alameda city average
- Comparable to California state averages
- Significantly safer than neighboring Oakland
- Less safe than elite East Bay suburbs (Danville, San Ramon, Pleasanton)

**For Bay Area context:** This is considered a "safe enough" neighborhood. It's not the safest area in the Bay (those would be places like Danville, Orinda, or Pleasanton), but it's well above average for the region and has active community safety measures.

**Primary recommendation:** Use the garage for vehicles (don't park on the street), be aware of package theft, and engage with the Harbor Bay Isle community security resources.
